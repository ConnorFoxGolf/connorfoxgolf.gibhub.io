About

My name is Andrew and I am a computer science student at the University of Michigan's College of Engineering. I have passions for programming, learning new things, and fitness and it is my goal to use these passions to positively impact the lives of others. My academic interests lie in artificial intelligence, autonomy, and robotics. I am currently exploring these interests through the Crowds and Machines (CROMA) Lab advised by Dr. Walter S. Lasecki and the Michigan Mars Rover Team (MRover). In the CROMA Lab, I am researching hybrid intelligence systems (combining human and artificial intelligence) and human-computer interaction. On MRover, I am a senior advising member and former lead of the autonomous navigation subteam. In my free time, I enjoy hockey, college football, and sub-par golf.
